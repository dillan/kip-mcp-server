# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want this project to be a friendly, harassment-free place for everyone, whatever your
experience level, background, or identity. Everyone taking part — contributors, reviewers,
maintainers, and people opening issues — agrees to keep it that way.

## What we expect

- Be kind and patient. Assume good intent, and explain your reasoning.
- Welcome questions, including from first-time contributors.
- Give and accept feedback gracefully; critique the code, not the person.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.

## What's not okay

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory remarks of any kind.
- Personal or political attacks.
- Sexualised language or unwelcome attention.
- Publishing someone's private information without permission.
- Anything else a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, and commit messages —
and whenever someone is representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behaviour that breaks these rules, please report it privately to the
maintainers through GitHub (open a private
[security advisory](https://github.com/dillan/kip-mcp-server/security/advisories/new) if you
need a confidential channel, or contact a maintainer directly). Reports are taken seriously and
handled discreetly.

Maintainers may remove comments, commits, or contributions that break this code, and may block
repeat or serious offenders from the project.
